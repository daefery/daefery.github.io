Portfolio Copy Approach

Which narrative structure best represents your brand?

A

Impact-Led Narrative

Lead every section with outcomes and numbers. Hero opens with "32M+ lessons. 5 companies. 7 years." Each role and project anchors on what changed, not what you did. Metrics-first, story-backed.

Best for: Recruiters, senior roles, data-driven orgs

Trade-off: Can feel cold without warmth injected carefully

B

Expertise Pillars

Organized around 3 pillars: Web Engineering · EdTech · AI-Assisted Dev. Clean sections, skill depth indicators, portfolio grouped by domain. Structured and scannable.

Best for: Freelance clients, agencies, technical audiences

Trade-off: Less memorable, harder to show growth arc

C

Growth Arc Story (Recommended)

Opens with mission: "I build software that teaches the world." Tells the journey — D3 Telkom → 4 companies → Senior Eng powering 32M lessons — as a human story. Impact numbers appear as proof points, not headlines. Warm, authoritative, memorable.

Best for: All audiences — jobs, freelance, competitions, personal brand

Trade-off: Needs strong copy discipline to stay tight, not bloated

My recommendation: C — then embed A's impact numbers inside C's story. You get the emotional hook AND the credibility metrics. Approach B works as a sub-section within C (skills breakdown), not as the top-level frame.
